Subhamon Supantha

Pre-doctoral Researcher at IIT Bombay

I am currently a pre-doctoral researcher at the Center for Machine Intelligence and Data Science (CMInDS), IIT Bombay, working under the mentorship of Prof. Parthe Pandit. We are currently working on the theory of overparameterized neural networks in the interpolation regime. Before this I was at the Chennai Mathematical Institute where I completed my MSc in Computer Science. My Msc thesis, a survey on Neural Tangent Kernels was supervised by Prof. KV Subrahmanyam.

I had spent a wonderful summer at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Mumbai where I worked with Prof. Abhishek Sinha on a problem in Online Learning.

My broader goal is to contribute to the theoretical foundations of modern machine learning, particularly in understanding why deep learning methods have such a surpisingly low generalization error. I am also particularly interested in the theory of online learning algorithms in both the adversarial, uncountable domain setting (Online Convex Optimization) and the stochastic finite setting (Bandit Convex Optimization).

My research interests lie in Deep Learning Theory and Sequential Decision Making. I aim to understand the foundations of modern machine learning and decision processes.

Research Interests

Deep Learning Theory, Neural Tangent Kernels, Sequential Decision Making, Online Convex Optimization, Bandits.
